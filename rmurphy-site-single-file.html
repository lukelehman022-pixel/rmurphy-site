01 · ADU Units

Accessory dwelling units, built to rent, house family, or add value

From garage conversions to detached new-builds, we handle design coordination, permitting, and construction as one scope — not three separate headaches.

[NEEDS: photo — detached ADU exterior]
Detached ADUs

A fully separate structure on your lot

Built from the foundation up, sized to your lot's setback and coverage limits. Includes its own utility connections, kitchen, and bath — ready to rent or house family independently.

  • Site survey and setback verification
  • Foundation, framing, and full MEP rough-in
  • Permit submission and inspection coordination
[NEEDS: photo — garage-to-ADU conversion, in progress or finished]
Garage Conversion ADUs

The fastest path to a livable unit

Converting an existing garage is typically faster and less expensive than a ground-up build, since the structure's shell is often already in place. Scope usually includes framing, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, insulation, drywall, and flooring.

  • Electrical service upgrade where needed
  • New plumbing runs for kitchen/bath
  • Mini-split HVAC sized to the unit
[NEEDS: photo — finished ADU interior]

Room Additions

Foundation-to-roof structural tie-in

New foundation and framing engineered to tie cleanly into your home's existing structure — matched roofline, matched load path, no visible seam.

Rather than describe our process in the abstract, here's what an average project actually looks like — using one real job as the example.

Before / After
Typical Timeline
Stage 01 [NEEDS: duration, e.g. "1–2 weeks"]

Site Visit & Proposal

We walk the space, discuss scope and budget, and send a written proposal with itemized costs and allowances — no verbal estimates.

Stage 02 [NEEDS: duration]

Contract & Permitting

Once the proposal is signed, we finalize the construction agreement and file for any required permits before mobilizing on site.

Stage 03 [NEEDS: duration]

Site Prep & Demo

Temporary facilities go up, demo begins, and debris is hauled off. This is when the mobilization draw is invoiced.

Stage 04 [NEEDS: duration]

Framing & Structural

Framing goes in for any new walls, ceilings, or structural tie-ins, inspected before it's covered by drywall.

Stage 05 [NEEDS: duration]

Rough-In: Electrical, Plumbing, HVAC

All in-wall systems are run and inspected before insulation and drywall close the walls.

Stage 06 [NEEDS: duration]

Insulation & Drywall

Walls and ceilings are insulated and closed up — the space starts to look like a finished room.

Stage 07 [NEEDS: duration]

Finish: Flooring, Cabinetry, Doors, Paint

Allowance selections (flooring, doors, cabinetry, fixtures) get installed once you've approved each one in writing.

Stage 08 [NEEDS: duration]

Final Walkthrough

We walk the finished space together, note any punch-list items, and close out final payment once they're resolved.
